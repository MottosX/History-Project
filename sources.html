Technical Documentation & Specifications

Technical specifications, performance data, and detailed analysis of weapons systems and their operational characteristics throughout American military history.

Weapons Performance Analysis

Data regarding firing rates, accuracy, range, and battlefield effectiveness compiled from historical testing and contemporary military reports.

  • Brown Bess Musket: .75 caliber smoothbore, 3-4 rounds per minute ideal conditions, 2 rounds per minute battlefield conditions, effective range 75 yards
  • Springfield Model 1855: .58 caliber rifled musket with percussion ignition, effective range 300-400 yards with Mini√© ball ammunition
  • Henry Repeating Rifle: .44 caliber lever-action, 15-round tubular magazine, 28 rounds per minute maximum rate with experienced operator
  • Spencer Repeating Rifle: .56-56 Spencer rimfire, 7-round magazine, 14-20 rounds per minute sustained combat rate
  • Colt Walker Revolver: .44 caliber, 6-shot cylinder, 60-grain powder charge, effective range exceeding 100 yards
"My God, we thought you had a division here!" - Confederate soldier's reaction to the 21st Ohio Infantry's sustained rapid fire using Colt revolving rifles at Chickamauga, demonstrating the force-multiplier effect of repeating weapons.

Technological Evolution

  • Smoothbore Era (1720-1830): Round ball projectiles, flintlock ignition, linear tactics
  • Rifling & Percussion Revolution (1820-1850): Spin-stabilized bullets, percussion caps, extended range
  • Repeating Weapons Era (1850-1865): Metallic cartridges, magazine feeding, rapid sustained fire
